All contributions are equally valuable to the community. There are several ways you can contribute to the library. Fix outstanding issues with the existing code.Submit issues related to bugs or desired new features. Contribute to the examples or to the documentation. Reference the library in blog posts and shout out on Twitter every time it has helped you. If you feel like you know what you're doing, go for it and we'll help you get there! If you're unsure where to start, there is a special Good FirstIssue listing. It will give you a list of issues that are beginner-friendly and help you start contributing to open-source. For something slightly more challenging, you can also take a look at the Good Second Issue list. The library is robust and reliable thanks to users who report the problems they encounter. It also helps us if you spread the word! Reference thelibrary in blog post or shoutout on Twitter. It has made a huge difference in the lives of people all over the world. For more information on how to contribute, visit the library's GitHub page. It's free and open source, and you can sign up for a free account to help support the project.
